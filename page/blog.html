Our Certificate & Online Programs For 2022

The data shows that Web Development makes up the largest portion at 30%. This means that nearly one-third of the opportunities or posts are focused on building and maintaining websites. It covers both front-end and back-end tasks, requiring skills in programming, design, and modern frameworks. Closely related to this, Front-end Development accounts for 20%, focusing mainly on the visual side of websites and user interfaces. Front-end developers work with technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to ensure websites are responsive, interactive, and user-friendly. On the other hand, Back-end Development makes up 25%, emphasizing server-side work such as database management, server logic, and application programming interfaces (APIs). This shows that both front-end and back-end development together form a large part of the demand in IT careers.

Moving beyond web development, the Network category has the smallest share at 5%. Network specialists ensure reliable connectivity, security, and performance in computer systems, which is vital but often requires fewer positions compared to development roles. Meanwhile, Software Development accounts for 10%. Unlike web development, software developers focus on creating applications for desktops, mobile devices, or enterprise systems. They often work with languages like Java, Python, or C#. Similarly, IT Support Specialists also represent 10%. These professionals provide essential troubleshooting, technical support, and system maintenance, ensuring smooth day-to-day operations for users and organizations.
